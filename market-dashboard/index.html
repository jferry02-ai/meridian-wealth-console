5 Macro Trends

this week
1. Cooler CPI cuts hike odds — but Warsh isn't declaring victory
Rates

Fed funds steady at 3.50%–3.75%. June CPI surprised to the downside — 3.5% YoY headline (vs. 3.8% expected) and 2.6% core, down sharply from May's 4.2% three-year high — pulling the probability of a July 28–29 hike from 42% to roughly 17%. New Chair Kevin Warsh, testifying before Congress for a second day, dropped traditional forward guidance in favor of pure data-dependence and warned one soft print doesn't mean inflation is beaten.

Why it matters: rate path is the single biggest input to duration/asset-allocation calls, bank NIM forecasts, and DCF discount rates — the most common technical thread pulled in AWM interviews.
Sources: Bloomberg, Schwab, Forbes
2. The Great Rotation — value catching up to growth
Rotation

The mega-cap tech/growth names that led 2025 into early 2026 are cooling as higher-for-longer rates compress the present value of far-out cash flows. Financials, healthcare, and energy — laggards during the AI rally — are outperforming as investors reposition for a higher-rate regime.

Why it matters: sector rotation calls are core to equity research and portfolio construction pitches; be ready to name which sectors you'd overweight and why.
Source: GoMarkets — US market drivers, July 2026
3. AI capex supercycle tops $1T — increasingly debt-funded
AI / Capex

The five largest hyperscalers are on pace to spend over $1 trillion combined on AI infrastructure from 2025–2026, with spend now outpacing free cash flow — pushing firms toward bond issuance. The buildout is shifting from digital (chips/software) to physical (data centers, power). Fresh confirmation Jul 15: ASML (the sole maker of EUV lithography tools) beat Q2 estimates on "extremely strong" AI chip order intake and raised full-year 2026 revenue guidance to €43–45B from €36–40B, plus a 30% capacity expansion plan. Goldman Sachs estimates AI infrastructure investment will drive roughly half of S&P 500 earnings growth in 2026.

Why it matters: the "monetization vs. pure capex" debate is the defining equity narrative of 2026 — have a view on whether AI spend is creating durable earnings or overbuilding risk.
Sources: Oxford Economics, Goldman Sachs, Rappler — ASML
4. Tariff cliff — Section 122 global tariff expires July 24
Trade Policy

The White House's 10% global tariff (invoked under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974) took effect February 24, 2026 for 150 days and is set to expire July 24, 2026 unless Congress extends it. Separately, USTR Section 301 investigations are expected to yield new, more targeted tariffs in Q2/Q3 2026. Europe's manufacturing sector is lagging on earlier tariff drag while the U.S. rebounds on AI spend and fiscal expansion.

Why it matters: tariff policy directly shapes multinational earnings guidance and currency/hedging conversations — a live risk factor to flag in any 2H26 outlook discussion.
Sources: Avalara, Oxford Economics

Sound Like a Pro

recruiting prep
If asked "what's happening in markets right now?"

Lead with today's disinflation surprise: June CPI cooled to 3.5% YoY (core 2.6%), well below estimates, cutting July hike odds from 42% to ~17% — though Chair Warsh is cautioning it's one data point, not a victory lap. Layer in the record $49B bank earnings quarter and ASML's AI-driven guidance raise as evidence the "higher-for-longer-but-still-growing" backdrop is intact, then pivot to the July 24 tariff deadline as the next swing factor for 2H26.

Six numbers to know cold
  • Fed funds rate — current policy stance and where the market thinks it's headed.
  • 10-year Treasury yield — the "risk-free rate" anchor for every DCF.
  • 2s10s spread — yield curve shape; inversion has historically preceded recessions.
  • VIX — the market's fear gauge / implied volatility.
  • High-yield OAS (credit spread) — how much extra yield junk bonds demand over Treasuries; a real-time read on risk appetite.
  • DXY (Dollar Index) — dollar strength, which drives multinational earnings translation and EM flows.
Concepts worth having a POV on
  • Soft landing vs. stagflation — can the Fed cool inflation without a recession, given inflation is still running above target?
  • Duration risk — why long-duration growth/tech stocks are more rate-sensitive than value/cyclicals.
  • Sector rotation — the shift from growth (tech) into value (financials, healthcare, energy) as rates stay elevated.
  • AI monetization vs. overbuild — is $1T+ in hyperscaler capex generating durable earnings, or creating stranded-asset risk?
  • Lock-up expiry — why newly public stocks (e.g., SpaceX) face predictable selling pressure around insider unlock dates.
  • Multiple compression/expansion — how discount-rate changes move valuations independent of earnings.
Daily habit stack (10–15 min/day)
  • Skim WSJ Markets or FT headlines + CNBC's "Stock market today" live blog for the prior close.
  • Check the 6 numbers above (10Y, 2s10s, VIX, HY OAS, DXY, Fed funds) — most are on a single FRED or CNBC page.
  • Note one Fed speaker headline and one earnings beat/miss — both are easy interview small talk.
  • Know the top 3 holdings and AUM scale of the shop you're interviewing with (BlackRock, Vanguard, Fidelity, Morgan Stanley IM, JPM AM, Goldman AM) — sizing context matters in AWM specifically.
Questions to ask your interviewer
  • "How is the desk/team thinking about positioning for a Fed that's dropped forward guidance in favor of pure data dependence?"
  • "With AI capex now roughly half of projected S&P earnings growth, how does the team underwrite monetization risk versus pure infrastructure spend?"
  • "How does the tariff expiration on July 24 factor into how you're advising clients on international/multinational exposure right now?"
